Rank One

Rank One is a badminton spec comparison application. It's a place where you can meet all of your badminton racket needs in one place. Search through all available rackets to see which racket suits your needs the best and upgrade your badminton game play!

The Problem

Purchasing rackets can be a huge purchasing decision that many may not know how to approach. By helping consumers be more informed about the rackets they want to purchase, they will feel more empowered to make the big purchase and improve their game.

Badminton players face several challenges when shopping for rackets. Technical specifications like stiffness, weight, balance point, and stringing tension significantly impact performance, but comparing these specs across different brands and models is time-consuming. Players often visit multiple websites, read scattered reviews, and struggle to make side-by-side comparisons. This fragmented shopping experience leads to decision paralysis and sometimes results in purchasing the wrong racket for their playing style.

The Solution

Since there is no centralized place to compare the specs of rackets, I decided to create a place for which you can. I created separate views in which you can compare and browse through rackets you search for or add to your view.

Rank One streamlines the racket selection process by aggregating comprehensive specifications from all major badminton racket brands in one intuitive platform. The app empowers players to make data-driven decisions through powerful comparison tools, flexible sorting options, and an interface designed around how badminton players actually shop for equipment.

Key Features & Design Decisions

Sort by Feature - Prioritize What Matters Most

User Scenario: If the user is trying to sort by the qualities they value the most

Design Solution: I added a sort by feature, which would help you sort your rackets that you add based on price, stiffness, weight, and stringing tension.

This feature recognizes that different players have different priorities. A beginner might prioritize price, while an advanced player might focus on stiffness and weight. The sorting functionality allows users to quickly reorganize their comparison view based on the specifications that matter most to their playing style and budget.

Browse by Brand - Leverage Brand Loyalty

User Scenario: If the user prefers a certain brand of rackets that they usually play on

Design Solution: I added a screen in which the user can browse by brand. This design choice balances what the user wants and efficiency. If the user doesn't know what kind of rackets they want, they can just be more general searching by brand.

Many badminton players develop preferences for specific brands based on their playing experience. Some prefer Yonex for its technology, others trust Victor or Li-Ning for their quality. The browse-by-brand feature caters to this behavior while also serving as a discovery tool for players who want to explore all offerings from a manufacturer they trust.

Effortless Comparison Management

User Scenario: If the user changes their mind about what rackets they want

Design Solution: I made the comparison view include easily accessible buttons for adding a racket (one tap) and removing a racket from their view (one swipe).

The racket selection process is often iterative. Players might start with five rackets, narrow down to three, then add two more for comparison. The interface needed to support this fluid exploration without creating friction. The one-tap add and one-swipe remove interactions make it effortless to refine your comparison set, encouraging users to experiment and thoroughly explore their options.

Information Architecture

The app is structured around three core user flows:

Search & Discover: Users can search by racket name, browse by brand, or explore curated collections based on playing style (offensive, defensive, all-around).

Compare: Add rackets to a comparison view where specifications are displayed side-by-side in an easy-to-scan format. Key specs like weight, balance point, stiffness, and recommended tension are prominently displayed.

Decide: Sort and filter the comparison view to surface the best options based on individual priorities. Quick access to detailed reviews and purchasing options helps users move from research to purchase seamlessly.

Design Principles

Clarity Over Complexity: Badminton racket specifications are technical, but the presentation shouldn't be overwhelming. Visual hierarchies and clear labeling make specs accessible to both beginners and advanced players.

Flexibility in Exploration: Players approach racket shopping differently. Some know exactly what they want; others need to explore. The app supports both directed search and open-ended browsing.

Frictionless Interaction: Every tap should move the user closer to finding their perfect racket. Minimizing the steps between discovering a racket and comparing it was a core design goal.

Data-Driven Decisions: Empowering users with comprehensive, accurate specifications helps them make confident purchasing decisions and ultimately improve their game.

Impact & Future Enhancements

Rank One addresses a genuine need in the badminton community by centralizing racket information that was previously scattered across manufacturer websites, forums, and retail platforms. The app empowers players to make informed purchasing decisions, potentially saving them from costly mistakes and helping them find rackets that truly match their playing style.

Potential Future Features:

  • Personalized recommendations based on playing style and skill level
  • Community reviews and ratings from verified racket owners
  • Price tracking and alerts for favorite rackets
  • Integration with retailers for seamless purchasing
  • Racket history and gear management for tracking personal equipment

By consolidating the badminton racket shopping experience into one comprehensive platform, Rank One helps players spend less time researching and more time on the court.
