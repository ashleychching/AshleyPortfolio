In 2020, just as the pandemic swept the world, I set out to pursue my dream of building something from scratch. My quest is to build a badminton app that would help players track their rankings and tournament results. I wanted to create a platform that would make it easier for players to find tournaments, register for them, and track their progress.

Competitive badminton players often struggle to track their rankings and tournament results. The existing solutions were fragmented and difficult to use. The problem

For years, I've had a dream to build a product—but where do you even begin? I struggled to find a starting point. The ideation stage was tough, and I needed to find a problem worth solving. I spent months researching and talking to potential users to understand their pain points.

So at the time, I was playing a lot of badminton. I noticed that players were struggling to track their rankings and tournament results. The existing solutions were fragmented and difficult to use. Players had to visit multiple websites to find tournaments, register for them, and track their progress. This was a time-consuming and frustrating process.

I realized that there was an opportunity to create a platform that would make it easier for players to find tournaments, register for them, and track their progress. I wanted to create a platform that would be simple and intuitive, with a focus on the user experience. But I also knew that I needed to validate my idea before investing too much time and money into it.

That's when I turned about Lean Startup. Screw it, I decided to just go ahead and build it. I didn't have all the answers, but I knew that I had to start somewhere. I decided to build a minimum viable product (MVP) and test it with real users. I wanted to see if there was a demand for the product before investing too much time and money into it.
